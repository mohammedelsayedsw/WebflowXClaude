the problem

Your customer is split. By market, and by tool.

Growth into new markets adds store views, languages, and assortments. Each one gets its own data, its own tools, its own version of the customer. Personalization stops at every border.

1

Data siloed by market

Separate store views and customer databases that never resolve into one person. The shopper who buys in one market is a stranger in the next.

2

A tool for every job

Email in one platform, recommendations in another, ads in a third. None share a profile, so a segment built in one channel cannot be used in the others.

3

Paying more for less

Tools priced for scale but capped on the automations you actually want. Spend keeps rising while relevance stays flat, and reporting lives in five places.
